Header image: Charles Livingstone, "Photographic Scene in an African Village," [c.1857-1865], detail. Copyright National Library of Scotland. Creative Commons Share-alike 2.5 UK: Scotland. Although such images might purport to document nineteenth-century cultural and material realities in Africa, the images were in fact often highly staged by European photographers and so serve much more like documentary evidence about those photographers, their objectives, and the expectations of their audiences than about the African individuals who serve as the subjects of the images. Indeed, the images might be said to mute those African subjects rather than giving them any sort of voice.
