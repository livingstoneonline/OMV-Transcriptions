Bibliography

The following bibliography compiles primary and critical sources related to the One More Voice project. If you know of additional sources we should include, please email us. Users may also want to consult Livingstone Online’s more general, but comprehensive bibliography.

Primary sources mainly date to the nineteenth-century, include print and manuscript sources, and fall into one of three categories:

  1. Authored (broadly defined) by the historical figures that form the focus of our project;
  2. Inclusive of reported speech and/or other summarized data from these historical figures; and
  3. Useful contextual sources related to the historical figures (these sources are marked “Contextual source” at the end).
Names and other information in square brackets have been supplied. The form of attribution used to identify authors of and contributors to the primary sources will continue to evolve as our project evolves.

Critical sources discuss relevant historical figures individually and in general and/or take up issues related to the study of relevant materials. The boundary between primary and critical sources can be gray as, for instance, some critical sources include critically-edited primary materials.
