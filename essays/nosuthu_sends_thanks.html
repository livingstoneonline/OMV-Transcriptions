Header image commentary: The image caption provided by the University of Southern California Digital Library reads as follows: “The Incehra [i.e., Ncerha] mission, in the Eastern Cape, was renamed Lovedale [in] 1826 in memory of Dr John Love (1757-1825) and was later resituated on the Tyumie [Tyhumi] River in 1837. The mission ministered to the Xhosa speaking people and the mission became famous for its educational endeavours.” No image of NoSuthu is known to survive, although Joanne Ruth Davis, author of the essay above, recently turned up one of Tause, NoSuthu's daughter and possible amanuensis for NoSuthu's letter, that is now included in the One More Voice Artifacts section. However, the header image here helps frame NoSuthu's letter historically as the image speaks to the cultural contexts in which she lived and worked and, moreover, gives some indication as to how these contexts evolved into the twentieth century.
