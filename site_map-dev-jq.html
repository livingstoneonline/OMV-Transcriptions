Header image commentary: One More Voice publishes a selection of textual items associated with Wainwright, a British-educated African traveler who accompanied Livingstone during the last two years of the latter's final journey. These include an item list added to one of Livingstone’s diaries, a set of original diary pages that only surfaced recently, a handful of letters, an inscription carved in a tree trunk, and a previously overlooked autobiographical narrative from the Victorian periodical press. The items have never before been collected in such a fashion and, in fact, represent a rare instance where such a set of African-authored texts, as produced by someone associated with a significant Victorian-era British explorer, can be assembled. The fact that most of the items survive in Wainwright’s hand makes them even more valuable as a historical resource, as comparable items are often found only in mediated, third-party versions.
