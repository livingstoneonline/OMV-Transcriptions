Overview  top

One More Voice collects and curates a small selection of relevant, publicly-available motion pictures and, in a few cases, sound recordings. These items offer an opportunity to see and hear some of the individuals who feature elsewhere on the One More Voice project site, including on the pages for archival texts, artifact images, and book-length works. For historical context, the present page also includes a few early anti-colonial and other documentary films as well as a handful of dramatic performances. To suggest additional items, please email us.

Header image: Anonymous Vili Artist, “Male Figure (Nkisi Nkondi)” (Figure, early to mid-nineteenth century), Ada Turnbull Hertle Endowment, 1998.502, Art Institute of Chicago, Chicago. Courtesy of Art Institute of Chicago. CC0 Public Domain. Communities in the Congo used such figures, which include ingridients for providing supernatural powers, to punish individuals guilty of wrongdoing. Each nail represents an instance of the figure's inhabiting spirit being summoned. Although One More Voice focuses on recovering non-European written contributions from nineteenth-century British imperial and colonial archives, such writings represent only one form of expression by the individuals and communities taken up by the project. Historical footage and recordings can be equally as important in this regard as can material objects (such as this figure) that form part of the wider intellectual and cultural contexts of the writings. Such objects mights illuminate relevant, culturally-specific ways of knowing (cf. Fong 2018) or provide less mediated alternatives to One More Voice texts like, for example, a series of informant statements collected by British Consul Roger Casement in the Congo Free State in 1903. Note: The Art Institute of Chicago, which holds the original of this figure, provides an interactive online exhibition that further outlines the history and significance of this figure.
