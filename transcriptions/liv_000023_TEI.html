Hare & chief.

Fable African - A chief offered ten goats to
anyone who could sleep in a Lake - Hare did
5lie in water all night & his mother made a
fire beside Lake to warm him on coming out
chief refused goats because of this - Hare
made a feast putting meat in one pot -
Pombe in another & flour mixed with
10Pombe in a third - made a fire in the midst
& invited all the chief men to feast - chief
heard of strange feast uncooked & came to
ask the meaning - Hare told him that the
feast was being cooked in the same fashion
15as he was warmed by his mothers fire
chief acknowledged justice of simile &
gave him his goats -


Hare is supposed to have been a man
and for some crime was transformed
20into a hare - Retains some of his
human wisdom - great   cunning
is ascribed to him &     Jackall

Fable African - Hare & Hyaena made
friendship - lived on opposite sides of
5a stream - Hare in time of great hunger
proposed to eat their mothers & wash off
the blood in river - Hyaenas mother was
first devoured then Hare stole a goat &
passed meat off for his mother - Famine
10continued - Hare went to foot of large tree
with a dwelling on it called & his mother gave
him food - Nyane told Hyaena Hare had
his mother there - tried to call her, but his
voice was harsh - charm was to let the
15biting driver ants ^ bite [            ] his tongue till his
voice became like Hare's were captured
took Hyaena to Beehive - MatupaFisi
or nyuki Fisi - stung him all over -
bound Hare with Muale &

Lion & = Lion went to visit the pig and

the pig - this he did 3 often but Hyaena
said how foolish ye pigs are - We have

rain & held stone up - then by excuse
10got lion to take his place in holding it
and fled - Lion afraid that house would
fall held for many days - then came
Hyaena –     Kayele     Myalitope

Warori or Basango = History of

A foreigner arrived in Urori
from the South West coast having
six attendants and a quantity of red
coral beads - He was light coloured
5had a prominent ^large nose and a
profusion of long hair. During
his stay the Warori went three
times to fight with another tribe
and each time waswere beaten off -
10The stranger whose name was
Charura asked about this fighting
and percieved that they needed a
war charm - for which he desired
the chief to give an ox & a goat -
15divide the carcases   equally betwe[  ][en]
himself and the stranger -       This
being     done Charura invited all
the people who chose to come &
eat his share.     The charm given
20proved successful and the
Warori returned laden with
captives and spoil - Every time
they went to plunder the same
process was repeated and all
25^the spoil was divided
The people eat the sacrifices - &
        all his half spoil too
Charuras six men died and
so did the Warori chief -     The
30people then said Why should we
make chebren our chief - Let us
have Charura who is a wise
and generous man as our

Monyegumbe died about 10 years
ago was then younger looking
than his sons -
head & ruler - A son Maakasii was
5born to Charura and turned out
to be a fool who preferred cooking
and domestic life to everything else
In process of time this foolish
son got a son himself who was
10called Monyegumbe and a wise
man The grandfather sent him
on his forays which were always
successful, and always were
accompanied with great
15feasting for all as was Charura's
custom and is still in vogue
        lived upwards of 100 years &
Monyegumbe is the father of
Merere and only the royal
20family dare wear the coral

Charura is said to have had a
