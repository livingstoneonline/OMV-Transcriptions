Curated Historical Artifacts

The artifacts on this page comprise photographs, manuscripts, and other historical objects that somehow illuminate or otherwise help fill out the historical and cultural contexts of the critically-edited archival texts and book-length published works collected by the One More Voice project. The gallery below is arranged alphabetically by subject and for each item provides a thumbnail image and brief description. Each thumbnail image, in turn, is linked to the full documentary record for the given artifact as well as a larger-resolution, uncropped version of the artifact image.

Used by permission for private study, educational or research purposes only. Semane Khama is seated in the foreground of this image; Tshekedi Khama stands second from the left in the back row. Although One More Voice nominally focuses on the “long” Victorian era, i.e., 1837 to 1914, the project is by no means bound by these dates or even by this way of defining the historical period in question – an interpretive approach that echoes recent critical work focused on “undisciplining” the field of Victorian Studies. As a result, the project's temporal remit can accommodate a 1934 letter from Semane Khama to a representative of the London Missionary Society (coming soon), more so as the letter illuminates geopolitical relationships first established between inhabitants of Bechuanaland (now Botswana) and Britain in the nineteenth century.
