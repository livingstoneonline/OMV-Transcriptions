Curated Artifacts

Cite page (MLA): Wisnicki, Adrian S. “Curated Artifacts.” One More Voice (an imprint of Livingstone Online), site launch edition, 2020, https://onemorevoice.org/artifacts.html.

Terms of use: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International

Header image: Detail from Weatherly Poulett and [Jacob Wainwright], “Dr. Livingstone, May 4 1873; Inscription Carved by African Youths on the Tree in Central Africa at the Foot of Which Livingstone’s Heart Was Buried” (Photograph, c.1895-1896), no. 561464i, Wellcome Library, London. Copyright Wellcome Library, London. Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International. Weatherly took this photograph just a few years before the original inscription was removed from the tree and transferred to the Royal Geographical Society in London. The inscription, one of the more unusual items published by the One More Voice project, underscores the fact that the non-European sources with which the project engages survive in a wide variety of forms and genres. Note: Livingstone Online publishes a version of this inscription, as reproduced in a magic lantern slide.
