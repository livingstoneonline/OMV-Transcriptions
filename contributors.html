Header image commentary: In addition to writing from Livingstone (throughout the page, rendered in various shades of blue) and the British Consul at Zanzibar John Kirk (at center of the page, rendered in black), this page contains an additional five lines of text (rendered in dark gray). Three of these are in Arabic and two in Nagari, the conventional script for Sanskrit as well as Hindi and Marathi. Further discussion of this page can be found among the critical materials developed by the Livingstone Spectral Imaging Project. The page offers a good example of how a manuscript produced within the context of the British Empire and normally attributed to a single author (in this case, David Livingstone) can in fact contain unattributed writing from others, including non-European individuals.
