Header image: Anonymous illustration of “Saleh Bin Osman” from Saleh Bin Osman, “The Story of My Life,” trans. [E. J. Glave], St. Nicholas: An Illustrated Magazine for Young Folks, May 1891. Public domain image provided by HathiTrust; courtesy of the University of Michigan; digitized by Google. One More Voice publishes two textual items associated with Saleh Bin Osman, an Arab-African who traveled with the British-American explorer Henry M. Stanley. Both items, like this image, are not originals but rather survive in mediated, third-party versions. The first, a Testimony from 12 November 1890, has been “carefully translated from KiSwhahili” by E.J. Glave, a British explorer. The second, a published autobiographical narrative that accompanies this header image, notes that the narrative is presented “[a]s told by Saleh and recorded in shorthand.” As a result, both texts (and this image) exemplify an interpretive point central to the One More Voice project, namely that such sources require a cautious, critical approach due to their mediated nature and cannot be taken at face value.
